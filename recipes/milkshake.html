Milkshake Recipe

Description

This is a recipe for my favorite sugar-free mocha milkshake recipe. The recipe uses actual coffee, so make sure to use a caffeine-free alternative if serving it for children or people who don't drink caffeinated drinks. The recipre is also sugar-free and uses sugar alternatives instead, so make sure that the people that you're serving it to are not allergic or sensitive to those ingredients.

I like to use real coffe in my mocha milkshare because I first came up with the recipe while wanting to drink a milkshake, but also needing caffeine desparately. I've had mocha milkshakes before this of course, but they were usually only coffee flavored, not actually caffeinated.

There are many different options for sugar substitutes these days. My favorite ones to use are allulose and stevia. I usually use allulose for recipes where I need the sugar substitute to melt, but since that is not neccessary for this recipe, I usually just use stevia.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sugar-free chocolate ice cream
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder/li>
  • 4 tbsp sugar alternative
  • 1/4 cup strong coffee (chilled)
  • whipped cream (optional)

Steps

  1. Make and then cool down your coffee.
  2. Combine ingredients.
  3. Use a stick blender to blend everything together.
  4. Serve in glasses with whipped cream on top.
